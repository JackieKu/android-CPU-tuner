profiles

Profiles are systemwide settings that are applied by triggers. At the moment (v. 2.0) there's two categories, you can adjust:
  • CPU settings
    By setting the virtual governor or the CPU governor and its parameters.
    These settings determine the CPU's clockspeed ("how fast it is") and power consumption.
  • services
    These settings can toggle some system services on and/or off.
    As said in battery drainers it's wise to disable syncing, wifi, etc. on some states, but you should not switch services too often.
    You can leave [the service in question] unchanged, but you may as well enable or disable services with triggers, additionally you can go back to [the] previous state which activates the state the service was in before the last switch. Furthermore you can pulse a service, in this case it will be switched off for some time (e.g. 15 min), then automatically switched on for a (short) period (e.g. 1min) and then the cycle begins again. All pulsed services will be switch on and off at the same time.
    If you manually override these settings, e.g. cause CPU tuner disables sync at 20% battery, but you need to sync you calendar with Google, this setting will stay untouched by CPU tuner unless a new trigger is activated and modifies the service's state.
